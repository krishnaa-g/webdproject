BARBARIKA – GRANDSON OF BHIMA

Barbareek/Barbarika is one of the sons of the Asura Putra of Bheem, Ghatothkach. Even though he wasn’t the most powerful or the mightiest warrior of his time, he happened to have not ONE of the most powerful weapons, but three. He had the three infallible arrows which was granted to him by Mahadev, and the bow which was bestowed to him by Agni Deva. It was so powerful that the three arrows alone could destroy humanity.

So you can see that if he had participated in the war along with the Pandavas then he could have destroyed the entire Kauravas in a minute. In fact he wanted to join the war, but he wanted to join the side that was weaker, that is, the one with the less number of army factions(less Akshauhinis). That was a oath he took. That means it should be fulfilled.

The problem was that, if he joined the Pandavas and destroy the Kauravas then the Kauravas will become the weaker side and hence he would have to destroy the Pandavas also, leaving no man at the end of the war but him.

So when Krishna told him about this consequence and advised him to stay away from the war, he could not oblige as it was an oath he took. The only solution to this was that that he should die. So Krishna requested him to be allowed to slay off his head so as to fix the problem. Barbarika was happy to be able to be of a use to his grandfathers (Pandavas) and Krishna, but on a condition that he should be able to watch the whole Mahabharata even after he was beheaded. And hence it was done. He was one the people who got the opportunity to watch the whole war. His case was a bit special as he was able to watch the war with his eyes even after his head was detached from his body. His head was kept on a mountain somewhere near to the Kurukshetra so that he could comfortably watch the war.

ARJUNA, DURYODHAN AND THE FIVE GOLDEN ARROWS

Duryodhana had gone to Dwaita forest to humiliate the Pandavas with all the servants from Indraprastha. There a Gandharava by the name Chitrasena, and his apsaras were enjoying in the pond. Duryodhana asked Chitrasena to abandon the pool but as he refused, a heated argument broke out between them. Chitrasena attacked Duryodhana.

The Pandavas who were nearby asked Chitrasena to let Duryodhana alone. Chitrasena who was a friend of Arjuna spared Duryodhana but not before tying him with vines and setting him on his horse. Duryodhana though humiliated, thanked Arjuna and asked him what he wanted for the turn he had done to him. Arjuna said that he would ask from Duryodhana some other time.

At the time of war, Bhishma, who was on the side of Kauravas was accused by Duryodhana for doing no harm to the Pandavas and favoring them. On hearing this, Bhishma became very angry and in his anger, he took five golden arrows and filled it with mantras.

He vowed that the Pandavas would die before the Sunset. But Duryodhana suspected that Bhishma would never do anything which would harm the Pandavas. So, he took those arrows with him.

Lord Krishna reminded Arjun that Duryodhana had promised him of boon and he should ask him for the five golden arrows. When Arjun asked him about the arrows Duryodhana was shocked and asked him how did he know about those arrows then Arjun replied “Krishna”.



SARVADA-SON OF BHIMA

Bhima had one surviving son named Sarvaga by Balandhara, who was not given the throne despite being much elder to Parikshit. He became the ruler of Kashi (his mother’s land) instead.It is curious that Sarvada did not fight the Mahabharata war while King of Kashi fought for the Pandavas. While Abhimanyu was killed in the war, Sarvada's non-participation may have removed him from contention to the throne of Hastinapur.

It is possible that since the King of Kashi may not have had male heirs, he was chosen as the male heir to the royal lineage and abdicated his right to the throne of Hastinapur.

It is also possible that ruling over large empires was difficult in olden days and for the purpose of good governance kingdoms respected each others boundaries.

DID VIDURA POSSESS THE MOST POWERFUL EVER WEAPON?

We all know that Krishna came to Hastinapur on behalf of the Pandavas for peace negotiation. He had a warm welcome from the Kaurava side (extraordinary archery welcome from Karna). He disappointed Duryodana by refusing his request to stay in the house arranged by him and told him that he will stay along with Vidura’s family for the entire duration of visit. He already knew that Duryodana would have arranged someone to monitor Krishna’s activity in his palace if Krishna accepted to stay in. So he decided to go for an alternative.

The very next day during the time of peace negotiation, Duryodana abuses Vidura for giving Krishna(their enemy) a place to stay , with harsh words, accusing him of partiality and favoring Pandavas' side, forgetting that Vidura was an elder in the family and his own paternal uncle.

After a point of time, Vidura's rage exceeded when he heard Duryodana abusing him on the basis of his birth and cast aspersions on the character of Vidura's mother as well.

Immediately he burst out like thunder, taking out his bow and arrow, aiming at Duryodhana. But Krishna cleverly intervened and played a trick. Turning towards Duryodhana, he said, “O Kaurava king, do not provoke Vidura like this. If you do, he might break his bow and declare that he will not fight on your side and would be neutral.”

Duryodhana, however, was still indignant and arrogant and said it did not matter if Vidura decided against fighting.“ Without his help, I (Duryodhana) can win the war.” At last, an enraged Vidura was unable to digest any further insult. He declared, “Krishna, you guided me on the right path and reminded me of my power of archery. I will fight no more.” So saying, he strung his bow and broke it with a thunderous noise accompanied by a flash of lightning, Vidura thanked Krishna and walked out of the durbar.

DRAUPADI’S PLEA TO SHIVA

Shiva was really scared because he knew what Draupadi asked for, was actually possible but this will disturb the very happening of Mahabharat. He kept quiet for a while and then told her that it was just impossible. What she was asking for, was not possible for him to give it to her as a boon. Draupadi was not happy with Shiva.

Shiva promised her that he will instead give her five men with five qualities with one quality in each. In this way,she will get what she wanted. Draupadi told that she wanted all qualities in single person. She had given her soul to such a man.If she would not get her boon,she will not be happy with Shiva.

Shiva told Draupadi that man is a human.He can possess only certain qualities.He cannot have all qualities. This was impossible.If he have it all,where would he put those qualities? What she was asking for, was against the rules of nature. Shiva tried to convince her and asked her to accept those five men because she had asked for them actually. It is her mistake to ask for a boon which was not present with Lord Shiva.
